**Solar Energy Generation Forecasting using Data Analysis & Machine Learning**

## 2.  Executive Summary

This project analyzes and predicts solar energy generation based on environmental and operational data. By leveraging machine learning models, it aims to improve energy forecasting accuracy, enabling better planning and optimization of renewable energy resources.

---

## 3.  Business Problem

Renewable energy sources like solar power are highly dependent on environmental conditions. Inaccurate predictions can:

* Lead to inefficient energy distribution
* Increase operational costs
* Cause instability in power supply

The objective is to build a predictive system that forecasts solar energy output, helping energy providers optimize production and grid management.

---

## 4.  Methodology

* **Data Collection**: Solar plant generation data + weather data (temperature, irradiation, etc.)
* **Data Preprocessing**:

  * Handling missing values
  * Time-series formatting
  * Feature scaling
* **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)**:

  * Trend analysis over time
  * Correlation between weather and energy output
* **Feature Engineering**:

  * Time-based features (hour, day, season)
* **Model Building**:

  * Linear Regression
  * Random Forest Regressor
  * Time Series models (ARIMA / Prophet)
* **Model Evaluation**:

  * RMSE (Root Mean Squared Error)
  * MAE (Mean Absolute Error)

## 5.  Skills Demonstrated

## 6.  Results & Business Recommendation

* The model effectively captures solar generation patterns based on weather conditions
* Key influencing factors:

  * Solar irradiation
  * Temperature
  * Time of day

###  Recommendations:

* Use forecasting models for daily energy planning
* Integrate weather forecasts to improve prediction accuracy
* Optimize grid load balancing based on predicted output
* Support decision-making for energy storage usage
